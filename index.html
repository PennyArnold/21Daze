Make A Change

Studies say it takes at least 21 days to create a habit. Sometimes getting started is the hardest part, but really, it’s all hard. It takes more than just a deep desire to effect real life changes. It takes accountability and community.

Every time you set a goal, you make a promise to yourself. Each time you meet that goal, you gain confidence and develop the ability to overcome.

Sometimes we set goals, and we fail. Maybe we aren’t resolved, or maybe we get discouraged when the effort becomes boring or overwhelming. It is important to know that as long as you are breathing, you can always hit the reset button. Be kind to yourself. Each day provides new opportunities to do things a little differently. It is not easy to make changes, but it is possible.

If you have things that you want to change about yourself, never give up. Life is too short. You have incredible value and you have the potential to live a life of purpose and meaning.

Body

Move 15 minutes daily, run a mile, eat veggies, drink 8 glasses of water, take the stairs, eat a salad, cut out sugar or give up softdrinks. Quit drinking or smoking.

Relationships

Drop a toxic relationship. Set healthy boundaries. Reconnect with someone. Spend quality time with your kids. Find an accountability partner.

Mind

Read a little each day. Cut back on TV or social media. Take a class. Learn a new language. Take on a new hobby.

Career

Set up a dream board. Work on your communcication skills. Take a business course or tutorial. Make peace with a co-worker.

Spirit

Pray or meditate each day. Join a support group. Find a church. Seek truth. Serve others. Learn to be honest with yourself. Find margin in your life.

Financial

Analyze your spending. Develop a budget. Give away what you don't need to someone who does. Live on less. Communciate financial plans with loved ones.

Take the 21 Day Challenge

Join us in learning to make and keep promises to ourselves, making small changes to improve the quality of our lives. Start small. Work on one thing at a time. In taking this step, you are holding yourself accountable and joining a community of others who are working toward the same goal. This is a safe place to receive and give encouragement to others as we move from strength to strength.
